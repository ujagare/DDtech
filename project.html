Coupler Selection

Brochure coupler types, explained by project need.

Type H

When the continuation bar can rotate

Type H couplers are used where the continuation bar can be rotated at site after the coupler is fully threaded to the fixed bar.

Type F

When the bar is long or heavy

Type F couplers are used where the continuation bar is too long or heavy to rotate and final engagement is completed by screwing back the coupler.

Practical Benefit

These options give engineers and contractors flexibility based on access, bar handling, and site conditions instead of forcing one installation method everywhere.

Execution Workflow

How brochure information translates into site-ready execution.

The DD Enterprises profile references a practical sequence from bar preparation through threading and final assembly. We have split that into a clearer website-friendly flow below.

01

Bar-End Preparation

Rebar ends are cut perpendicular to the axis to prepare the bar properly for threading and fit-up.

02

Threading Process

Machine threading is completed to the required length and profile so the coupler can engage reliably in workshop or at site.

03

Assembly & Use

Depending on the coupler type, installation is completed by rotating the continuation bar or by controlled coupler movement.

04

Inspection & Support

Quality checks, site guidance, and practical execution support help the final installation stay dependable and project-ready.
