Profile Specifications

Standards, use-cases, and coupler performance.

Key points from the DD Enterprises profile including standards references, Type H and Type F coupler usage, and practical project applications.

Standards and performance details.

ACI 318-95

Full mechanical connection should develop at least 125% of specified yield strength in tension or compression.

BS 8110 Pt: 1997

Permanent elongation after loading to 0.6 fy should not exceed 0.1 mm for the representative assembly.

Type H Coupler

Used when the continuation bar can be rotated and engaged at site after the coupler reaches the end of the fixed bar thread.

Type F Coupler

Used when the continuation bar is too long or heavy to rotate and engagement is completed by screwing back the coupler.

Key Benefits

Cost savings against lapping, continuity of reinforcing bars, reduced congestion, reduced steel wastage, no torque wrenching, easier concrete flow, and preservation of 100% rebar cross-section area.

Application Areas

Where these systems help most.

Heavy Reinforcement Zones

Reduces steel congestion in columns and core walls and helps reduce honey-comb formation in concrete structures.

Bridge and Staged Construction

Useful in traffic-congested bridge projects where staged construction is needed to maintain movement and safety.

Special Use Cases

Suitable for diaphragm walls, starter bars, micropiles, and full tension splice connections between precast and cast-in-site members.
